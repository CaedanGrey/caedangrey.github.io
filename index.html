Anchorage

Nestled between rugged mountains and the shimmering waters of the Cook Inlet, Anchorage is Alaska's bustling metropolis. As the largest city, it offers a mix of urban comforts and untamed wilderness. Anchorage serves as a gateway to outdoor adventures, with its picturesque parks, trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Visit Anchorage

Juneau

Perched on the edge of the Gastineau Channel, Juneau stands as the capital city of Alaska, enveloped by a backdrop of lush forests and soaring peaks. Accessible only by air or sea, this charming city blends history with nature, offering glimpses of Alaskan heritage at every corner. Glaciers, whale-watching, and vibrant arts scene are just a few highlights.

Visit Juneau

Skagway

Tucked away in the Inside Passage, Skagway whispers tales of the Klondike Gold Rush with its well-preserved historic buildings and streets. This small town, surrounded by dramatic fjords and towering mountains, echoes with the footsteps of prospectors from the past. It's a gateway to wild beauty and a fascinating glimpse into Alaskan history.

Visit Skagway

Alaska Facts

Alaska, known as the Last Frontier, is a land of wonder and natural beauty. Here are some intriguing facts about this remarkable state:

  • Alaska is the largest state in the United States, covering over 663,000 square miles.
  • Denali, also known as Mount McKinley, is the highest peak in North America and resides within Alaska's boundaries.
  • The Northern Lights, also called the Aurora Borealis, can be seen in the Alaskan night skies during winter.

Alaska was officially admitted as the 49th state of the United States on January 3, 1959.
