About Skagway

Skagway, a charming town of about 1000 residents, allures nearly one million yearly visitors with its rich history, unique architecture, scenic railway, and walkable layout. This

It is recommended to stay for about 1-2 days in Skagway. There are many events to do such as ride the White Pass & Yukon Route Railroad, go to Liarsville Gold Rush Camp, or do Flightseeing events such as glacier landings where you can go dogsledding.

Alaska gem offers an array of attractions, including the National Historic Park, leisurely boardwalk strolls, and glimpses of its collective green spaces. Midnight Sun Excursions and Rainbow Glacier Adventures provide enticing tours, while the White Pass & Yukon Route Railroad offers a thrilling ascent. Helicopter dog sledding with TEMSCO Helicopters adds excitement, and downtown walking tours with the Skagway Audio Guide immerse visitors in the Gold Rush era. Adventure-seekers can tackle the legendary Chilkoot Trail, a 33-mile journey retracing gold seekers' steps. Skagway seamlessly blends history, natural beauty, and exploration.
